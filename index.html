About

About

I am a UX Designer, self-taught Frontend Developer, and Researcher with a degree in International Affairs. In short, I am a lifelong learner who loves a side quest. If I was in a historical drama I am sure it would be said that I am "prone to flights of fancy". I changed my major a few times in college before going back to my original one but I took valuable skills from each one. Years of bouncing around social science departments have taught me how to write a thorough research report; that and my curiosity help me write descriptive UX case studies. I am also a cycler of crafty hobbies, I've long enjoyed knitting, cross-stitching, painting, and sketching. I am an excellent researcher because I approach it with the same zeal I have for starting a new hobby.


Designing user interfaces calls for a minimalistic approach so as not to overwhelm a user with unnecessary features. When I worked as a hotel receptionist at a family-owned Wyndham hotel. I experienced firsthand how frustrating it is to use poorly designed software that is overloaded with useless features. Because of that day-to-day frustration of using a program that required 5 steps to do a task that should only take two, I strive to design software that is intuitive and free of unnecessary features. I am however a maximalist in my interior design with a passion for color blocking. That part of myself appears in my designs in my use of eye-catching color schemes and animations which elevate the site's UI.



I am a graduate of Thinkful’s UX/UI design boot camp. To complete my three projects, I used Figma and Sketch to create user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity prototypes. I also used Adobe Illustrator to draw logos and other illustrations for my deliverables. When I started the program I set a goal for myself that rather than use a website builder, I was going to write the code for my portfolio website myself. To that end, I have been self-studying HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and learning by doing. Continuing to work hard every day to achieve that goal even when it got difficult showed me that I have tenacity.


I am new to the field of UX design and new to coding as well, but I have a love of learning and I do not fear a challenge. I am excited to see where my journey in this field takes me!
